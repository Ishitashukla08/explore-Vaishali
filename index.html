About Vaishali

Vaishali is one of India's most historically significant districts, known as the world's earliest republic and the birthplace of Lord Mahavira. Rich in Buddhist and Jain heritage, the district beautifully blends ancient history with modern rural life.

History

For over 2500 years, Vaishali has stood as a symbol of democracy, peace, and spiritual learning. It was the capital of the ancient Licchavi Republic, visited by Gautama Buddha and remembered as the birthplace of Lord Mahavira.

Key Tourist Attractions

Discover some of the most iconic landmarks that make Vaishali a destination of history, spirituality, and culture.

Ashokan Pillar

A magnificent sandstone pillar erected by Emperor Ashoka.

Abhishek Pushkarni

A sacred coronation tank where ancient Lichchavi rulers were anointed.

Buddha's Relic Stupa

An ancient mud-brick stupa housing the sacred cremated remains of Lord Buddha.
